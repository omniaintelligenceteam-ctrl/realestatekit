The Property

A grand, century-old home, thoughtfully proportioned for modern family life.

Set on a generous corner of North Eighth Street in Paducah's storied Lower Town, this 1907 residence carries the kind of architectural detail that simply isn't built anymore — original hardwood floors, formal millwork, soaring ceilings, and the warmth that only comes from a home that has been lived in well, for well over a century.

Across nearly 4,724 square feet, the floor plan offers five bedrooms, five and a half bathrooms, multiple gathering rooms, and the kind of square footage that allows a young family room to grow and a working family room to host. A three-car garage anchors the rear of the property — rare in a neighborhood this established.

The 9,583 sq ft lot delivers room for gardens, a brick courtyard retreat, and a separate carriage house with a one-bedroom apartment above the garage — all within a five-minute walk of downtown Paducah and the riverfront.
