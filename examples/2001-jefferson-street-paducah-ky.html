About this home

A timeless piece of
Paducah charm.

Nestled along one of Paducah's most iconic and historic streets, this classic home blends early-20th-century character with modern livability.

Architectural presence, generous proportions, and enduring craftsmanship abound. The spacious living areas — defined by tall ceilings, original woodwork, and abundant natural light — create a warm and inviting atmosphere throughout, with a thoughtful layout ideal for both everyday living and entertaining.

The kitchen and baths provide a balance of updated functionality and classic style, while the bedrooms offer comfortable retreats with ample space and character — hardwood floors, fireplaces, and detailed millwork carrying the home's craftsmanship from room to room.

Sitting on a well-positioned lot with mature trees, sidewalks, and a strong sense of neighborhood continuity, it's just minutes from downtown Paducah and offers convenient access to local dining, shopping, and the city's celebrated arts scene — with a quiet, established feel.

The neighborhood

Historic Paducah,
moments from everything.

Paducah sits where the Tennessee River meets the Ohio — a UNESCO Creative City of Crafts & Folk Art with a downtown of brick storefronts, river-walk murals, and a thriving arts district. Jefferson Street is one of its most storied addresses: tree-lined sidewalks, sense of continuity, and that particular quiet that only old neighborhoods seem to keep.

From the front walk, downtown Paducah, the National Quilt Museum, and the LowerTown Arts District are all only a few minutes away.
