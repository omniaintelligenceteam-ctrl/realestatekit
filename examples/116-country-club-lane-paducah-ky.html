About this home

A 1913 cottage,
three owners deep.

Tucked into the desirable West End of Paducah, this beautifully preserved cottage-style home sits on a rare 0.94-acre lot — built in 1913 and lovingly cared for by just three owners across more than a century.

Inside, the bones tell the story: hardwood floors, exposed wooden beams, and a striking fireplace anchor the living room. The kitchen pairs granite countertops with ample cabinetry — and a window framed entirely by the garden beyond.

Step outside and the lot opens into something rare — a well-maintained English-style garden, mature flowers and shrubs forming a quiet, private retreat. The screened-in porch is the seat for all of it.

Four bedrooms, three full baths, and a 2-car detached garage round out a home that is, plainly, one-of-a-kind. The kind of details and craftsmanship you simply can't replicate today.

The neighborhood

West End Paducah,
moments from everything.

The West End sits among Paducah's quietest, most established streets — sidewalks under old oaks, lots wide enough to breathe, and that particular calm that only old neighborhoods seem to keep. The Country Club of Paducah is a few doors down.

From the front walk, downtown Paducah, the National Quilt Museum, the LowerTown Arts District, and the Ohio River floodwall murals are all only a few minutes away.
