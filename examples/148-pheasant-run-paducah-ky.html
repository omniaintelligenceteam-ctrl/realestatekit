About this home

A 2018 luxury townhome,
settled into the pines.

A 2018 luxury brick townhome on 0.63 wooded acres — the arborvitae press in close on three sides, and the house settles in among them like it was planned that way.

Inside, the scale announces itself: twelve-foot ceilings, dark crown molding, rich hardwood and porcelain floors running through every public room. The living room opens through French doors to the outside, and a stone fireplace with a hand-crafted dark wood mantel holds the opposite wall. This is architecture that understands proportion.

The kitchen is the kind that earns its word count in a listing. Amish custom cabinetry in dark cherry. A granite island with a farmhouse sink. Pendant chandeliers overhead. The whole thing flows into a dining area and back out to the living room — one long, unhurried conversation at every meal.

The primary suite carries a double tray ceiling with exposed wood beams and a ceiling fan that turns quietly overhead. The en suite runs a rain shower behind frameless glass and a jetted soaking tub in the corner, double granite vanity, oil-rubbed bronze fixtures throughout. A tankless water heater means the hot water keeps up.

The neighborhood

Paducah, KY —
close to everything.

148 Pheasant Run sits in a quiet established residential area on Paducah's west side — wooded lot, private setting, and still only minutes from the city's best landmarks. The Country Club of Paducah is nearby; downtown is a short drive.

From the stamped concrete drive, Noble Park, the National Quilt Museum, LowerTown Arts District, and the Ohio River floodwall murals are all within ten minutes.
